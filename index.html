Clear guidance on NSFAS-funded accommodation and how to apply correctly

What this website is

This platform is an independent student support and educational website. It exists to help students understand the NSFAS accommodation application process, what is required, and how to avoid common application mistakes.

This website does not process applications. All NSFAS accommodation applications are completed on the official NSFAS accommodation portal.

What is NSFAS accommodation?

NSFAS accommodation refers to student housing that is funded or supported by the National Student Financial Aid Scheme for qualifying students at public institutions.

  • University residences
  • TVET college residences
  • NSFAS-accredited student housing

Approval depends on your NSFAS funding status, institution rules, and accommodation availability.

Who qualifies for NSFAS accommodation?

Students funded by NSFAS may qualify if they meet the following criteria:

  • Approved NSFAS funding status
  • Registered or provisionally registered at a public institution
  • Institution-approved or NSFAS-accredited accommodation
  • Compliance with NSFAS rules and timelines
NSFAS accommodation is not guaranteed and depends on availability.

Before you apply

  • Approved NSFAS status
  • Valid South African ID
  • Student number
  • Access to your myNSFAS account
  • Institution registration confirmation

Applying without these may result in delays or rejection.

How the NSFAS accommodation application works

  1. Apply for and receive NSFAS funding approval
  2. Register at a public university or TVET college
  3. Access the official NSFAS accommodation system
  4. Select available accommodation options
  5. Submit your application on the official portal

Common mistakes students make

  • Applying before NSFAS funding approval
  • Missing application deadlines
  • Choosing non-accredited accommodation
  • Ignoring institution accommodation rules
  • Sharing myNSFAS login credentials
